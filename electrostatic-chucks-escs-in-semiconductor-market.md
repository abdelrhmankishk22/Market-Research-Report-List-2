<p><strong>The Electrostatic Chucks (ESCs) in Semiconductor Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Coulomb Type</li><li>Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type</li></ul></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Electrostatic chucks (ESCs) are crucial components used in the semiconductor industry to hold and secure wafers during the manufacturing process. </p><p>Coulomb Type ESCs function by utilizing the attraction between opposite charges. They employ electrodes with positive and negative charges to create an electrostatic force that holds the wafer in place.</p><p>On the other hand, Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type ESCs use an alternative approach. They rely on the phenomenon of electrodynamic suspension to levitate the wafer, rather than using electrostatic attraction. This allows for the elimination of any charging-induced contamination and provides superior alignment accuracy.</p><p>Both types of ESCs play vital roles in the semiconductor market, ensuring stability and precision in wafer manufacturing processes.</p></p>
